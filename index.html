    Welcome to OpenSafeMap

    This site is designed to provide guidance on adding points to OpenSafeMap in order to capture and record incidents of harrassment. This initiative is inspired by International Womens Day and the high volume of harrassment experienced recently by women on the island of Bali. In honour of this day a uMap instance powered by OpenStreetMap has been set up to capture incidents of 'Stalking', 'Verbal' and 'Physical' abuse. OpenSafeMap will be reviewed for the inclusion of other types of incidents such as 'Theft', depending on the tools reception and its effectiveness.

    Map an Incident

    Step by step guidance on how to map an incident on OpenSafeMap

    1. Zoom in to the correct location where the incident occurred.

    2. Select the pencil icon in the top right corner to activate edit mode.

    3. Select the place marker icon in the top right corner to place a point on the map.

    4. Place the marker on the map where the incident occurred.

    5. The layer of the feature should be 'Harassment'. Please enter the type of incident, either 'Stalking', 'Verbal' or 'Physical' as the name.

    6. Please enter the following details as part of the incidents description:

    • Date
    • Time
    • Landmark
    • Group or Individual (Victim)
    • Group or Individual (Perpetrator)
    • Notes

    See below as an example:

    • Date: 2016-02-29
    • Time: 1500
    • Landmark: Where the road dips
    • Group or Individual (Victim): Individual
    • Group or Individual (Perpetrator): Group
    • Notes: Two men drove by on a motorbike and the passenger grabbed as they passed by.

    7. The location of the incident can be set manually by entering the coordinates which can be captured with a GPS using a mobile device.

    8. Hit the 'Save' button to add the point to the map and save the details of the incident.

    9. The message 'Map has been updated!' will appear at the top of the screen to verify that the point has been added.

    10. Click on the newly added place marker to view the details of the recorded incident.

    11. Click the 'More' button on the top left corner of the screen to access additional map features.

    12. Select the magnifying glass icon to expand the application options for editing data in OpenStreetMap. This can be used to add roads and buildings for areas that still have to be mapped.

    13. Click the 'iD' button to launch the iD Editor which is recommended for beginners. Please follow the guidelines below on how to sign up to OSM.

    OpenStreetMap

    OpenStreetMap (OSM) is a collaborative project to create a free editable map of the world. Two major driving forces behind the establishment and growth of OSM have been restrictions on use or availability of map information across much of the world and the advent of inexpensive portable satellite navigation devices.

    Sign up to OpenStreetMap

    14. 'Login' or 'Register' an account with OpenStreetMap.

    15. 'Start the Walkthrough' if this is the first time using the application or 'Edit Now' to start mapping.

    16. The iD Editor application provides satellite imagery as a baselayer which can then be mapped by tracing roads and buildings on top of.
